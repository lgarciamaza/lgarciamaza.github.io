REACTION iDA IN THE CONSTRUCTION OF VARIOUS TETRAHYDROQUINOLINE
DERIVATIVES. STUDY OF THEIR PROPERTIES AS REGULATORY AGENTS OF QUORUM SENSING IN Pseudomonas aeruginosa

DIRECTOR

CARLOS MARIO MELENDEZ GÓMEZ, Qco, Ph.D
carlosmelendez@mail.uniatlantico.edu.co

CO-DIRECTOR

ARTURO RENÉ MENDOZA SALGADO, Qco, BSc
arenemendoza@mail.uniatlantico.edu.co

Objective of the project

Synthesize tetrahydroquinoline derivatives via an effective synthetic pathway such as Diels-Alder imino to obtain potential regulatory agents of Quorum Sensing in P. aeruginosa to eliminate or limit virulence factors that promote antimicrobial resistance.

Scientific achievements

  • A number of 4-amide-THQ derivatives were obtained using an economic catalyst (p-TsOH) under simple reaction conditions with moderate yields (24-78%) using the iDA cycloaddition methodology. Thanks to spectroscopic techniques such as IR, one-dimensional (1H, 13C and DEPT 135) and two-dimensional (COSY, HSQC and HMBC) it was possible to identify the chemical nature of the synthesized compounds.

  • Results of synthetic antagonists (N-(2-(o-tolyl)-1,2,3,4-tetrahydroquinoline-4-yl)formamide) in bacterial growth inhibition assays in culture of P. aeruginosa showed that compounds with electron-withdrawing group (-F, -Cl and -I) could be considered growth inhibitors from MIC = 10 μg/mL, albeit with low inhibition percentages of 13. 6%, 11. 9%, and 9%, respectively.

  • On the other hand, the formation of Biofilm and Pyocyanin in THQ derivatives was evaluated to explore the scope and biological limitation of the synthesized compounds. These tests also found greater activity on the compound with electron-withdrawing group (-F) at higher concentrations, being this antibacterial agent the most effective of the series.

  • The halogenated tetrahydroquinoline compounds induced the bacterium P. aeruginosa to express moderate to weak Biofilm formation indices at the highest concentration evaluated (75 μg/mL), correlating with pyocyanin production at this same concentration, so there is the possibility that the biological activity of the compounds on the P. aeruginosa strain is determined by modulation of the bacterial QS.

  • In this project, a series of N-(2-(o-tolyl)-1,2,3,4-tetrahydroquinoline-4-yl) formamide derivatives described in previous studies were constructed. However, studies of these molecules as inhibitors of bacterial QS have not been reported in the literature so far.
