BIOTECHNOLOGICAL POTENTIAL OF RESTINGA MICROBIOTA: ANTIBIOFILM COMPOUNDS AND NOVEL PROBIOTIC CANDIDATES

DIRECTOR

ALEXANDRE JOSÉ MACEDO, Ph.D
alexandre.macedo@ufrgs.br

Objective of the project

This master’s thesis aimed to investigate the biotechnological potential of marine bacteria isolated from the Restinga ecosystem's sediment along the Southern Coast of Rio Grande do Sul, Brazil. The study evaluated the probiotic traits and capacity of these bacteria to produce bioactive compounds with antibiofilm activities against clinically relevant pathogens. Through this approach, the thesis contributes to our understanding of the potential of marine microbial diversity as a source of novel biomolecules and microbial candidates for biotechnological and antimicrobial applications.

Scientific achievements

The results from my Master's research conclude that:

  • The choice of extraction methodology can significantly impact the recovery of marine-derived secondary metabolites and limit the detection of bioactive compounds.

  • Co-culture strategies involving environmental bacteria and human pathogens can increase secondary metabolite production.

  • Species of the genus Pseudomonas sp. are promising model organisms for studying probiotic-like traits in nonconventional Gram-negative bacteria.

  • Nutrient limitation during cultivation plays a key role in regulating and optimizing the production of bioactive compounds.

  • Restinga ecosystems are underexplored reservoirs of microbial diversity with significant potential for discovering bioactive compounds.

  • Marine-derived bacteria can exhibit both probiotic traits and the ability to produce bioactive metabolites.

  • Polyhydroxyalkanoates produced by environmental bacteria may be an alternative class of biomolecules with antibiofilm properties.

  • Gram-positive pathogens, such as Staphylococcus aureus, appear to be more susceptible to extracellular bioactive compounds than Gram-negative bacteria.

  • Natural products derived from marine microorganisms may contribute to the development of antivirulence strategies that target bacterial biofilms.

  • The physiological state of Pseudomonas sputi MaRe06 may be influenced by environmental limitations, affecting its responses to external stress factors.
