Biography

Hello, I am Leidy García, a first-generation student from Barranquilla, the golden gate of Colombia. I’m a passionate scientist driven by curiosity and a deep desire to explore both the world and the intricate world of science, a journey fueled by my determination to break barriers and pursue knowledge.

My scientific journey began in 2017, and by 2022, I completed my undergraduate degree in Chemistry, overcoming the challenges posed by the COVID-19 pandemic. In my final year, I had the opportunity to carry out a research internship as a young researcher at the Organic and Biomedical Chemistry Laboratory, under the mentorship of Dr. Carlos Meléndez Gómez at Universidad del Atlántico. This experience culminated in the presentation of my research at the Latin American Congress of Chemistry in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil, thanks to the support of the Colombian Ministry of Science, Technology, and Innovation.

Following this, I expanded my expertise by working as an HPLC analyst in Analytical Chemistry at Universidad del Atlántico, where I contributed to the development and analysis of methods for medicinal products in the Atlantic Department.

Currently, I am pursuing a Master’s in Pharmaceutical Sciences at the Universidade Federal do Rio Grande do Sul in Porto Alegre, Brazil, with a CAPES scholarship from the Brazilian Government. My research focuses on Microbial Bioprospecting, under the guidance of Dr. Alexandre Macedo, within the Biofilm and Microbial Diversity Research Group. I am excited to continue exploring the complex interactions between microorganisms and their potential applications in pharmaceutical sciences.
